About

Built around the person being moved.

Global Mobility Group LLC is a United States company. We provide a platform for managing the people side of projects that cross borders, backed by a network of vetted providers in the countries where the work happens.

Our position

Cross-border work is usually handled in pieces, one country and one specialty at a time, which leaves the people running a project stitching it together themselves. We bring it into one platform, organized around the person and the project, with vetted local providers handling what has to be done on the ground.

3. Data we collect

  • Identity data: full name, date and place of birth, nationality, sex, marital status, passport and national identity numbers, and parents' names where a government form requires them.
  • Contact data: mobile number, preferred communication channel, email address, and residential address.
  • Family data: names and relationships of family members, and identity information for dependent minors, where an assignment involves them.

Where an organization engages us for its personnel, that organization provides us with the contact details of the individuals concerned so that we can carry out the work.

4. Purposes of processing

We process personal data to determine the requirements that apply to an assignment, to prepare and manage applications, to communicate about case status, to coordinate with licensed professionals, to verify identity when someone accesses their account, to comply with legal obligations, and to improve our services.

We do not use personal data for profiling, for automated decision-making producing legal effects, or for any purpose unrelated to the assignment.

5. Who we share data with

  • Licensed in-country professionals who prepare, review, and submit applications.
  • Government authorities to which an application and its supporting documentation are submitted.
  • The organization that engaged us, limited to case status and information they need to meet their own obligations. Personal identity documents are not shared with them.
  • Technology service providers, including hosting, database, messaging, and email providers, each operating under a data processing agreement.
  • Payment processors that handle service fees.

We do not sell, rent, or trade personal data. We do not share data with marketing companies, advertisers, or unrelated third parties.

We send one-time verification codes by SMS to users who request them when accessing their account. These are sent only in direct response to a user-initiated request.

We do not sell or share mobile numbers or SMS consent with third parties for marketing purposes. Mobile numbers collected for verification are used for that purpose and for service communications relating to a case, and for no other purpose.

With your consent, we may also send case-related messages by SMS, WhatsApp, or email to remind you of outstanding documents, notify you of status changes, and provide case updates.

You can opt out at any time by replying STOP to any message, by changing your contact preferences in the platform, or by contacting support@globalmobility.law. Reply HELP for assistance. Message and data rates may apply. Message frequency varies.

7. International data transfers

Global Mobility Group LLC is established in the United States. This service is cross-border by nature, and personal data may be transferred to and processed in the United States and in other countries outside the European Economic Area, including any country where a filing must be made.

Where we transfer personal data outside the EEA, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place in accordance with Chapter V of the GDPR, including Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the European Commission.

8. Data retention

  • Active cases: retained for the duration of the case and for 5 years after closure, to support renewals, appeals, or follow-on procedures.
  • Enquiry data: if you register but do not proceed, retained for 12 months from your last interaction.
  • Documents: retained for the same period as the associated case. We never retain originals and work only from copies.
  • Communication records: retained for the duration of the case plus 3 years.
  • Financial records: retained for 7 years as required by tax and accounting regulations.

When data is no longer required, it is securely deleted or anonymized.

9. Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have the right to access your personal data, to have it corrected or erased, to restrict or object to its processing, to receive it in a portable format, and to withdraw consent at any time.

Under the GDPR these are the rights of access (Art. 15), rectification (Art. 16), erasure (Art. 17), restriction (Art. 18), portability (Art. 20), objection (Art. 21), and withdrawal of consent (Art. 7(3)).

To exercise any of these, contact legal@globalmobility.law. We respond within 30 days.

If you are in the European Economic Area, you also have the right to lodge a complaint with the data protection supervisory authority in your country of residence.

10. Data security

  • Encryption of data in transit (TLS/SSL) and at rest.
  • Row-level security policies ensuring advisors access only assigned cases.
  • Access controls limiting data access to authorized personnel.
  • Audit logging of all data access, modification, and download.
  • Periodic security review and updates.

If you believe your data has been compromised, contact legal@globalmobility.law immediately.

Cookie Policy

Last updated: 28 March 2026

This policy explains how we use cookies and similar technologies on our website and platform, and how you can control them. It should be read together with our Privacy Policy.

1. What cookies are

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website or use an online platform. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and to provide information to site owners.

We may also use similar technologies such as local storage, session storage, and pixel tags. For simplicity we refer to all of these as "cookies" in this policy.

2. Who sets cookies

First-party cookies are set by Global Mobility Group LLC directly. They support essential functions such as keeping you signed in and remembering your preferences.

Third-party cookies are set by our service providers, and are used for analytics. Third-party cookies are subject to those providers' own privacy policies.

3. Cookies we use

Essential cookies (strictly necessary). Required for the platform to function and cannot be disabled. We use gm_cookie_consent (local storage) to remember your consent preferences. Legal basis: exempt from consent under the EU ePrivacy Directive (Article 5(3)) as strictly necessary.

Analytics cookies. We use Google Analytics (_ga, _ga_*) on the main website only, to measure traffic and site usage in aggregate and anonymous form. These are not used in the application platform. Legal basis: consent (GDPR Article 6(1)(a)). They activate only after you provide explicit consent.

No marketing cookies. We do not use marketing or advertising cookies. We do not track visits from advertisements or share data with third-party advertising platforms.

Cookieless analytics in the platform. The application platform uses analytics in cookieless mode, with no persistent tracking on the user's device. No analytics cookie is set in that environment.

4. Cookie consent

On your first visit a consent banner will ask for your preferences. You can accept all cookies, accept only essential cookies, or choose which non-essential categories to allow.

We do not use cookie walls. You can use the platform with only essential cookies enabled. Declining non-essential cookies does not limit access to any functionality.

Your consent preferences are stored in an essential cookie and remembered for 12 months.

5. Managing cookies

You can change your cookie preferences at any time using the cookie settings link in the site footer.

Most browsers also allow you to view, delete, and block cookies through their settings: Chrome (Settings, Privacy and security, Cookies), Firefox (Settings, Privacy and security, Cookies), Safari (Preferences, Privacy, Manage website data), and Edge (Settings, Cookies and site permissions).

Blocking all cookies, including essential ones, may prevent the platform from working correctly.

6. Data collected through cookies

Depending on your consent preferences, cookies may collect session identifiers and authentication tokens (essential), language and interface preferences (essential), pages visited and time on page (analytics), referring website or campaign source (analytics), device, browser, and operating system type (analytics), and IP address, which may be anonymized (analytics).

This data is processed in accordance with our Privacy Policy. Where analytics data is collected, we configure our tools to anonymize IP addresses and we do not combine analytics data with your personal case data.

7. Third-party services

Our platform may include links to or integrations with third-party services. Those third parties may set their own cookies when you interact with their services. We do not control third-party cookies, and their use is governed by those providers' own policies.

8. Do Not Track signals

Some browsers send a "Do Not Track" signal. There is currently no universally accepted standard for how websites should respond to these signals, and our platform does not currently respond to them. You can control your preferences through the consent banner and your browser settings as described above.

9. Changes to this policy

We may update this Cookie Policy to reflect changes in the cookies we use, the technologies we employ, or legal requirements. When we do, we will update the date at the top of this page. Where changes are material we will notify you through the platform and seek consent again where required.
